Understanding Uncertainty

Probability, densities, and inference — built from scratch, one knob at a time

A first course in the machinery of uncertainty for data scientists: from wrangling and linear algebra, through probability and densities, to simulation, the bootstrap, and the central limit theorem. Every idea arrives three ways — a paragraph, an equation, and something you can drag.

Interactive labs

Each lab is a single web page: a demo you can touch on arrival, a from-scratch derivation, and a Python assignment with a local autograder. More are being built out from the course notebooks.

Class 02 Vectors, Distance & the Inner Product Patients as points in space. One operation — multiply and add — underneath length, distance, covariance, and matrix multiplication. Class 01 Wrangling & Robust Statistics A salvage-title truck with over a million miles wrecks the mean but barely nudges the median. Real messy data, from schema to summary statistic. Class 03 Coins, trials & the binomial Bernoulli trials add up to the binomial. A PMF explorer, running-proportion sample paths, and counts converging to the PMF. Class 06 The CDF & the inverse transform The ECDF staircase, and how inverting it lets you sample any distribution from a single uniform draw. Class 07 Kernel Density Estimation A smooth curve where a histogram draws bars. One bandwidth knob, the bias–variance trade-off, and a KDE you build yourself. Class 09 Monte Carlo & the WLLN Watch a running average converge to the truth, and estimate π by throwing darts. Error that shrinks like 1/√n. Class 10 The Bootstrap Resample your one sample with replacement to simulate the sampling distribution and read off a confidence interval. Class 11 The Central Limit Theorem Averages of even badly-skewed data pile up into a bell — centered on the mean, tightening like σ/√n.
